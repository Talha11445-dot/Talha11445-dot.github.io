# My University Projects — Semester 1 🚀

During my first semester at UET Faisalabad, I worked on several 
exciting projects that helped me apply theoretical knowledge to 
real world problems. Here are the key projects I completed.

## USA Car Price Predictor 🚗

For our Programming Fundamentals final project, I built a Machine 
Learning model using the USA Car Dataset. I applied data analysis 
and predictive modeling techniques to real world automotive data. 
The project was presented to our instructor and received very 
positive feedback and praise.

This project introduced me to Python, Machine Learning basics and 
data analysis — skills that I am continuing to develop in Semester 2 
through Object Oriented Programming with C#.

## Physics Lab Experiments ⚗️

Throughout Semester 1, I conducted and documented multiple Applied 
Physics experiments. I maintained precise lab manuals, performed 
error analysis and applied theoretical formulas to real measurements. 
This experience taught me the importance of accuracy, documentation 
and attention to detail — skills that apply to both engineering and 
programming.

## Human Evolution Presentation 🎤

I led a group presentation on Human Evolution for the ICT course — 
my first major public speaking experience at university level. I 
focused on clear communication, visual aids and managing stage nerves 
with confidence. This experience greatly improved my public speaking 
and presentation skills.

## Calculus Problem Sets 📐

I solved every single problem set throughout Semester 1 with zero 
gaps. Consistent daily practice led to a perfect mid term score and 
an A grade in finals. I treated Calculus like an athletic training 
routine — no skipped reps, no shortcuts, just consistent hard work 
every single day.

## Technical Skills Gained

Through these projects and coursework, I developed skills in Python, 
C, Machine Learning basics, Object Oriented Programming concepts, 
Database Systems fundamentals and various soft skills including 
public speaking, technical presentations and team collaboration.

> "Every project is a chance to learn something new. 
> Build, fail, learn, repeat."
