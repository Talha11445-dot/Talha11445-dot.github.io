The university announced early this week that IBCC certificate verification needed to be completed by 5th June. IBCC, the Inter Board Committee of Chairmen, is responsible for authenticating certificates issued by different education boards across Pakistan, and missing a deadline like this isn't a minor inconvenience — it can result in holds on academic records or restrictions on exam registration. I made sure to get the process done well before the deadline rather than risk it colliding with exam prep later, which in hindsight was one of the more sensible administrative decisions I made all semester.

Right around the same time, our DLD teacher announced a lab quiz scheduled for 3rd June, covering all 14 labs from the entire semester — everything from basic logic gate verification at the very start, through combinational circuits like adders, multiplexers, and decoders, and on to sequential circuits including flip-flops, counters, and the Machine Safety Controller from the open-ended lab. Having that spiral-bound stack of labs from earlier in the semester turned out to be genuinely useful here — instead of trying to reconstruct half-remembered circuits from scratch, I could go back through actual documented diagrams and truth tables and rebuild my understanding properly rather than guessing.

Database Systems handed me a different kind of lesson this week, though not through difficulty — through disappointment. I'd prepared seriously and attempted a quiz with real confidence, only for our teacher to later decide to discard it entirely. It stung initially, mostly because the preparation had felt solid and the result evaporating along with the quiz felt unfair in the moment. But thinking it through afterward, I realized the knowledge I'd built while preparing hadn't actually disappeared anywhere — it resurfaced later in other exams and quizzes without me even consciously trying to recall it. It was a useful, if slightly frustrating, reminder to separate the intrinsic value of learning something from the extrinsic reward of a grade attached to it.

Between a bureaucratic deadline I managed to clear in time, a comprehensive lab quiz that rewarded months of documentation, and a quiz that taught me more by being discarded than it might have by counting, this week was less about new material and more about how differently effort can pay off — sometimes immediately, sometimes later, and sometimes in a way you only recognize after the fact.
