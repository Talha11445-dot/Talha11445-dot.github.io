Following the lab quiz from a few weeks earlier, our DLD teacher announced a theory quiz on 10th June focused specifically on Chapter 4 — Combinational Circuits. The scope was wide: circuit analysis and design methodology, half and full adders and subtractors, ripple carry adders, multiplexers, demultiplexers, encoders, decoders, and parity generators. It's the kind of chapter where each topic builds on the one before it, so revising it properly meant going through the material in order rather than jumping around to whatever felt most familiar. It demanded a level of focused, disciplined study that the breadth of the syllabus made unavoidable — there wasn't really a way to shortcut it.

Outside the classroom, UET's Sports Festa gave the semester a different kind of competitive moment. Our Computer Engineering cricket team faced off against Electrical Engineering in a match that stayed close for most of its length before ultimately ending in defeat. It wasn't the result anyone on the team wanted, but there's a particular kind of lesson that only shows up after a loss like that — sportsmanship, the value of teamwork under pressure, and the discipline of accepting defeat gracefully instead of dwelling on it. If anything, the loss left the team more motivated to train harder ahead of next year's Festa rather than less.

Between a demanding theory quiz that rewarded methodical revision and a cricket match that rewarded resilience more than the final scoreline did, this week was a reminder that not every lesson worth learning comes from a textbook or a lab manual — some of them come from losing a match you genuinely wanted to win.
