# My Sports and Fitness Journey 💪

Physical fitness is not separate from academic performance — it is the 
engine behind it. Since starting university, I have maintained a strict 
fitness routine that keeps me energized, focused and mentally sharp 
throughout my academic journey.

## Daily Running 🏃

I run 1 mile every single day, targeting under 8 minutes per mile. 
This habit started during my Day Scholar days at UET Faisalabad when 
I needed energy for long commute days. Running has improved my stamina, 
mental clarity and ability to handle exam stress and heavy workloads.

![Running](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1552674605-db6ffd4facb5?w=800)

## Gym Training 🏋️

I train regularly at the gym following a structured weekly routine. 
Each session is dedicated to a different muscle group to ensure a 
complete and balanced workout throughout the week.

![Gym](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1526506118085-60ce8714f8c5?w=800)

### Monday — Chest & Triceps
I start my week with bench press, chest fly and tricep pushdowns 
to build upper body strength and endurance.

### Wednesday — Back & Biceps
Mid week I focus on deadlifts, pull ups and bicep curls to strengthen 
my back muscles and improve posture.

### Friday — Legs & Shoulders
I end my gym week with squats, leg press and shoulder press to build 
lower body strength and overall stability.

## Badminton 🏸

Badminton is my favourite sport and I play it 2 to 3 times a week 
with my friends. It is fast paced, exciting and a great full body 
workout. Playing badminton has improved my reflexes, agility and 
hand-eye coordination while also being a great stress reliever 
after long study sessions.

![Badminton](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1626224583764-f87db24ac4ea?w=800)

## How Fitness Helps My Studies

These fitness habits helped me manage long commutes, exam stress and 
increasing workloads throughout Semester 1. The same discipline I 
apply to my daily run, I apply to my studies — consistent effort, 
no skipped sessions, and always pushing for improvement.

> "Physical fitness is the engine behind academic performance. 
> Run daily, train hard, never stop growing."
