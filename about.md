## Who I Am

I'm **Talha**, a 1st year Computer Engineering student at **UET Lahore (Faisalabad Campus)**, currently based in **Faisalabad, Pakistan**. This site is my digital footprint as a student — a running record of the labs, projects, courses, and lessons that made up my first year, written as I lived through them rather than cleaned up in hindsight.

## Where I'm Headed

I haven't fully settled on one path yet, and I'm keeping both doors open for now. On one side, I'm genuinely drawn to **AI/ML and databases**, and I can see myself growing into a Computer Engineer or AI Engineer, building on the database and machine learning foundations I started this semester. On the other side, I've also seriously considered the **Armed Forces** route — joining the Pakistan Army, Navy, or Air Force — which is part of why I made a Communication Skills vlog on the Physical Fitness Test for Armed Forces early in the semester. For now, I'm letting both interests develop in parallel rather than forcing an early decision.

## My Learning Journey This Semester

This semester covered a wide spread of courses — Object Oriented Programming, Database Systems, Digital Logic Design, Programming Fundamentals, Calculus, and Communication Skills — and each one pushed me in a different direction.

In **Object Oriented Programming**, I went from building a Library Management System early on to a full University Admission Management System, an Online Shopping System, a Car Racing Game, and eventually a comprehensive GUI-based quiz. Encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism stopped being lecture terms and became tools I actually reached for.

In **Database Systems**, I had probably the biggest shift in perspective of the whole semester — from assuming the subject would be "just theory" to genuinely falling for SQL the moment I saw a `SELECT` statement return exactly the data I asked for. That curiosity carried through a seismology dataset project, a Kaggle earthquake analysis, an ER Diagrams mid-term lesson, and finally topping Section B on the final exam.

In **Digital Logic Design**, I moved from basic gates and latches all the way to a Machine Safety Controller and a hand-built Digital Stopwatch, eventually binding and documenting all 14 labs — a stack that represents an entire semester's practical learning in one place.

In **Programming Fundamentals**, I learned a humbling but valuable lesson early on — that prior knowledge of one language doesn't automatically transfer to another — and worked my way back up through a hands-on Machine Learning project on the USA Car Dataset to a B+ I felt genuinely earned.

## Beyond Academics

Outside of coursework, I try to stay active and disciplined — fitness and physical training matter to me, partly because of my interest in the Armed Forces route, and partly because I just think a clear head needs a body that's kept up too. I also value consistency over intensity: starting exam prep early, using spaced repetition, and documenting my work as I go (like this very site) are habits I picked up this semester that I plan to keep.
